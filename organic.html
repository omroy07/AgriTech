🌿 Principles of Organic Farming

  • Ecology: Work with living systems and nutrient cycles.
  • Health: Sustain soil, ecosystem, climate, human well‑being.
  • Fairness: Equitable relationships between people & the environment.
  • Care: Responsible farming to protect future generations.

✅ Step-by-Step Organic Farming Process

1. Site Assessment & Soil Preparation

  • Analyze plot: drainage, soil depth, erosion risk.
  • Test soil for pH, nutrients, organic matter.
  • Improve fertility: green manure, compost, farmyard manure; reduce tillage for structure.

2. Crop Planning & Rotation

  • Select diverse crops based on microclimate, soil type & market.
  • Design rotation cycle (3–8 years) to interrupt pests & replenish nutrients.
  • Include legumes in rotation to fix nitrogen.

3. Soil & Nutrient Management

  • Use cover crops (clover, mustard) to prevent erosion and add biomass.
  • Enrich soil with vermicompost and biofertilizers.
  • Use conservation agriculture for long-term soil health.

4. Pest & Weed Control (IPM)

  • Monitor pest populations and set outbreak thresholds.
  • Use cultural controls: crop hygiene, trap crops, mulching.
  • Release beneficial insects; use botanical sprays (neem, garlic).

5. Efficient Water Use

  • Build rainwater harvesting structures like bunds and trenches.
  • Adopt drip irrigation & maintain soil moisture with mulches.
  • Avoid water logging and salinity issues.

6. Record-Keeping & Certification

  • Prepare an Organic System Plan (OSP): methods, materials, buffer zones.
  • Log all inputs, field operations, harvest, and transitions.
  • Explore Participatory Guarantee Systems (PGS) as affordable certification.

7. Marketing & Value Addition

  • Target premium buyers: co‑ops, organic stores, export markets.
  • Differentiate produce with transparency, farm visits, storytelling.
  • Explore on-site processing (e.g. cold-pressed oils) to increase margin.

🌾 Crop-Specific Organic Best Practices

Rice (SRI Method)

Transplant young seedlings (8–12 days) with wide spacing; intermittent wetting to reduce water use and enhance root growth.

Millets

Hardy, low-input crops. Intercrop with legumes to fix nitrogen and improve soil fertility.

Guar (Cluster Bean)

Heat tolerant crop; use FYM (25 t/ha) and biofertilizers at sowing; minimal care needed post‑establishment.

Cotton (Organic)

Include pest-repellent plants; apply biofertilizers. Chhindwara, MP’s organic cotton restored soil and profit.

Multi-Crop Systems

Adopt traditional intercropping like 'Barahnaja' (12+ species) to boost biodiversity and resilience.

📌 Case Study: Organic Wheat in Punjab

Sucha Singh Pabla transitioned 27 acres to organic wheat: input costs ↓, premium price ₹3,500/quintal, profit margin ~50%, no stubble burn – healthier soil & air.
